overview: "A soap brand conceived as an object of presence and meaning. Built for a male audience, the project draws from Mayan heritage to shape a contemporary identity where daily care becomes ritual, not routine."
summary: "The brand is rooted in origin. Inspired by Itzamná, a symbol of creation, balance, and wisdom, the identity translates ancestral values into a modern visual language. Strength and restraint coexist, allowing the brand to feel grounded without becoming literal."
additionalText: "The name Altar K’uh carries this intention. It connects archaeology, language, and symbolism into a single gesture that feels both cultural and current. From there, the design system emerges with clarity. A pyramid form is reduced to its essence, joined by an altar that completes the silhouette of an “A”. The mark becomes structure, not decoration.

Packaging extends the experience. It holds the same discipline, using form, contrast, and material presence to reinforce the idea of ritual. The result is a brand that does not imitate tradition, but builds from it, shaping a distinct identity that feels deliberate, composed, and enduring."
